The AI "Audition" Report

Finding the perfect small brain for Bluecrest's News Radar

1. The Job Description

We needed to hire an AI agent to sit in the basement and read thousands of news articles every day. Its specific instructions were:

"Read this article. If it's about Bluecrest Wellness, our competitors (Bupa, Nuffield), or corporate health trends, flag it as High Priority. If it's just general news, sports, or entertainment, throw it in the bin."

The Catch: It needs to be small enough to run on a standard laptop (under 4GB memory) and fast enough to read hundreds of articles per minute.

2. The Candidates & The "Hallucination" Trap

We auditioned several popular "small" models. We gave them a trick question: A news report about the New York Yankees winning a baseball game.

Qwen 2.5 (1.5B)

The "Over-Eager Intern"

Relevance Score: 7/10 (High)

Why? "The Yankees are a popular team, and fans celebrate games. Happy fans might want health screenings."

It tried way too hard to be helpful, finding connections that didn't exist.

Granite Code (3B)

The "Slow & Confused"

Relevance Score: 9/10 (Critical)

Why? "Our target audience likes sports."

Not only was it wrong, it took 4x longer to be wrong.

3. The Fix: "Prompt Hardening"

The models were too creative. To fix this, we updated their instructions with a strict new rule:

CRITICAL INSTRUCTION: "If the article is about a sports game or entertainment event and does not explicitly mention healthcare business, the score MUST be 3 or lower."

We ran the auditions again with this new rule.

Qwen 2.5 (Round 2)

Score: 6/10

Still failed. It argued: "Yankees share consumers with our target market, so it influences corporate values." It just couldn't let go.

Gemma 2 (2B)

Score: 1/10

It correctly ignored the baseball game, but it also ignored a real competitor threat, scoring it too low. It fell asleep on the job.

The Verdict

The Winner: Llama 3.2 (3B)

  • Accuracy: 100% on our test suite.
  • Speed: ~6 seconds per article (fast enough for background processing).
  • Size: 2.0 GB (Very lightweight).

Recommendation: Free vs. Paid?

Stick with the free, local model.

While a massive paid model (like GPT-4 or Claude 3.5 Sonnet) would be "smarter" in a general sense, Llama 3.2 is perfectly capable of this specific filtering task.

Paying for a larger model for this stage would be like hiring a PhD physicist to sort your mail. It would cost 100x more for the exact same result. We have configured your system to use Llama 3.2 by default.
